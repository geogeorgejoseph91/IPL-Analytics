# LinkedIn Post — IPL Analytics (First Power BI Project)

## Post Content

15 seasons. 950 matches. 296,000 runs.

I turned all of it into my first Power BI dashboard. 🏏📊

───────────────────

A few months ago I knew nothing about Power BI.

Today I built a full interactive IPL Cricket Analytics Dashboard — covering every season from 2008 to 2022.

Here's what the data revealed:

📌 Mumbai Indians have the highest win rate & most titles across 15 seasons
📌 Toss winners choose to field 60%+ of the time — the data backs the instinct
📌 Home advantage is real — home teams win significantly more often
📌 DA Warner (641 runs) & B Kumar (26 wickets) dominated the 2017 season

───────────────────

The dashboard has 3 interactive pages:

🔹 IPL Championship Trends (2008–2022)
🔹 Season Spotlight — 2017 deep dive
🔹 Team & Player performance explorer

Built using:
→ Power BI Desktop
→ DAX (measures & calculated columns)
→ Power Query for data cleaning
→ Data modeling, slicers & drill-through filters

## Post Tips

**Upload media (order matters):**
- Lead with IPL_dashboard.mp4 — video gets 3–5× more reach than images

**Tagging (do this — it multiplies reach):**
- Tag @Rakesh OV (find his LinkedIn profile) → he may reshare → his network sees it
- Tag @Luminar Technolab official LinkedIn page → institution may reshare

**Timing:**
- Post Tue, Wed, or Thu — 8–10 AM local time (highest feed activity)
- Avoid Mon morning and Fri afternoon

**After posting:**
- Reply to EVERY comment within the first 60 minutes — early engagement signals the algorithm to boost distribution
- Ask 2–3 friends to comment (not just like) immediately after posting
- Pin the post to your profile

**What drives reach on LinkedIn:**
- Comments > Likes > Reactions for algorithm weight
- The question at the end ("what stat would YOU dig into?") is intentional — it invites comments
